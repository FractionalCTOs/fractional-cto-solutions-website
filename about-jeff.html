Professional Biography

Jeff Wray is a distinguished technology leader who bridges the gap between technical innovation and business strategy. With an MBA in Finance and a Bachelor's in Computer Information Systems from Stetson University, Jeff brings a unique dual perspective that enables him to translate complex technical requirements into clear, business-driven outcomes.

As Director of Technology at Mobilozophy since 2009, Jeff is responsible for developing technology strategy and execution for the company's mobile marketing platform, APIs, professional services, and custom SaaS implementations. He has architected cloud computing solutions that accommodate high-volume, real-time transactional databases, and has led numerous web and mobile application projects for FORTUNE 100 companies across multiple verticals including Healthcare, Hospitality, Loyalty, Oil & Gas, Entertainment, Non-Profits, and Retail.

Jeff has developed particular expertise in complex API integrations with third-party platforms such as ExactTarget, social media platforms (Facebook, Twitter), messaging services (Twilio, Open Market), email marketing systems (MailChimp, Mandrill, Constant Contact), AWS infrastructure, and various loyalty and learning management systems. This extensive integration experience has been crucial in his work with enterprise clients like Shell Oil U.S., where he revolutionized loyalty, retention, and customer growth strategies through advanced integrations and real-time marketing solutions.

His approach to solving complex business challenges combines technical expertise with strategic business thinking. Jeff excels at turning around failing projects, optimizing infrastructure, and building high-performing teams. His leadership philosophy emphasizes coaching, inclusivity, and resilience—qualities that have been shaped by his personal journey overcoming significant physical challenges.

The Wray Family's Entrepreneurial Heritage

Three generations of innovation that have shaped Jeff's approach to technology and business

Proxix Solutions

2003 - 2007

Paul Wray, Founder

Became a leader in location intelligence with a focus on insurance underwriting and hazard determination. Acquired by First American Corporation in 2007.

Paul introduced one of the first parcel-based geocoders that utilized county-level parcel data to dramatically improve property geocoding accuracy, revolutionizing risk assessment in the insurance industry.

Hyphos360

2001 - 2004

Paul Wray, Founder

Formed after re-acquiring the database marketing services group of QMSoft/Sagent. Offered multi-channel marketing and analytics solutions to retailers and financial service providers. Acquired by Primus Marketing Group in 2004.

Qualitative Marketing Software (QMSoft)

1994 - 1999

Paul Wray, Founder

Became a leader in GIS spatial processing with clients including Allstate Insurance, Yahoo!, and MapQuest. Grew to 110 employees and $20M in revenue in just five years. Acquired by Sagent Technology in 1999.

A true visionary in the GIS field, Paul pioneered the integration of data with geographic information systems, building the first Windows-based geocoder that could fit on a CD, powering major platforms including MapQuest and Yahoo Maps.

Omni Data Inc.

Late 1970s - 1983

Paul Wray, Founder

Founded in the late 1970s, Omni Data developed the first real-time software fundraising system for major non-profit organizations. Acquired by Metromail Corporation in 1983, which later became part of Experian.

This revolutionary platform, known as COIN (Contribution Online Information Network), transformed nonprofit fundraising by enabling real-time donor data management when most systems still used overnight batch processing.

Monument Manufacturing Company

1934 - 1970s

Melvin C. Wray, Founder

Established in 1934 in Assonet, Massachusetts. During World War II, became the largest domestic producer of sleeping bags for the U.S. armed forces. Melvin invented the ironing board cover, a household staple across America.

Beyond manufacturing excellence, Melvin Wray was a marketing visionary. In the late 1940s, he founded what is considered the first business-associated quilting club, complete with a nationwide mailing list - an early example of database marketing.

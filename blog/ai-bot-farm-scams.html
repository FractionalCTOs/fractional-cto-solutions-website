The TCPA Compliance Test

Here's a simple test: Ask any "AI bot expert" about TCPA compliance for automated messaging. Real professionals will discuss consent requirements, opt-out procedures, and regulatory risks. Scammers will dismiss it as unnecessary worry or, worse, mock you for being cautious.

I've personally been called a "little woman" for raising TCPA compliance concerns. This from someone claiming to be an AI expert who doesn't understand basic telecommunications law. That's not expertise - that's dangerous ignorance that could cost your business millions in fines.

The White-Label Shell Game

These "experts" don't build technology - they resell it. Their "revolutionary AI bot system" is usually:

  • A basic GoHighLevel automation template
  • Simple Zapier workflows marketed as "AI"
  • ChatGPT API calls wrapped in a pretty interface
  • n8n workflows they discovered last month

They charge $5,000+ for what takes 30 minutes to set up, targeting businesses that don't know better.

How to Protect Yourself

  1. Check their history: Use Wayback Machine to see their website from 2 years ago. Were they the "Solar King" then?
  2. Ask technical questions: Real experts can explain technical concepts clearly
  3. Demand references: Not testimonials - actual clients you can call
  4. Test their compliance knowledge: If they dismiss TCPA/GDPR, run
  5. Look for education: Real tech experts have relevant degrees or decades of experience
  6. Avoid the "Kings": Legitimate professionals don't need cartoon crowns

The Bottom Line

When someone goes from "Solar King" to "Lead King" to "AI King" in 3 years, they're not an expert in anything except rebranding. Real expertise takes years to build, not a weekend certification course.
